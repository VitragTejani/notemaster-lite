✍️ What’s a SOAP Note (and Why Should You Care)?

🚨 Real talk: Most IMGs + med students don’t get hands-on SOAP note training but attendings may expect you to know it from Day 1. We’re here to break it down and make it super clear ✅

🔍 SOAP = Easy, Once You Know This:

  • S = Subjective: What the patient tells you (symptoms, history). Example: "I’ve had a sore throat for 2 days."
  • O = Objective: What you observe (vitals, exam, labs). Example: Temp 38°C, red tonsils.
  • A = Assessment: Your diagnosis. Example: Likely viral pharyngitis.
  • P = Plan: What’s next (tests, treatment). Example: Supportive care + fluids.

🛠️ Example: Watch How It’s Done 👇

Case: 65M with 3 days of fever + cough. No chest pain/SOB. PMH: Hypertension, Diabetes.
Temp 38.5°C, crackles RLL. WBC 14k. CXR: RLL infiltrate.

  • S: 65M with 3-day fever and productive cough. Denies chest pain/SOB.
  • O: Temp 38.5°C, HR 95, BP 130/80. Crackles RLL. WBC 14k. CXR: RLL infiltrate.
  • A: Community-acquired pneumonia (CAP), right lower lobe.
  • P: Start IV ceftriaxone + azithromycin. Repeat CXR in 48h.
